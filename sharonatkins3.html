HARDLY WORKING

Charlie Blossom

Ned Williams

You try to fill up your time with trying to think about other things: what you’re going to do on the weekend or about your family.

You have to use your imagination.

If you don’t have a very good one and you bore easily, you’re in trouble.

Just to fill in time, I write real bad poetry or letters to myself and to other people and never mail them.

The letters are fantasies, sort of rambling, how I feel, how depressed I am.

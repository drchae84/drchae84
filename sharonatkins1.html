HARDLY WORKING

Charlie Blossom

Ned Williams

You come in at nine, you open the door, you look at the piece of machinery, you plug in the headpiece.

That’s how my day begins.

You tremble when you hear the first ring. After that, it’s sort of downhill—unless there’s somebody on the phone who is either kind or nasty.

The rest of the people are just non, they don’t exist.

They’re just voices. You answer calls, you connect them to others, and that’s it.
